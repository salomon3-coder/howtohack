faq:
  - question: "How often should I back up my personal files?"
    answer: "For most home users, a daily or weekly automatic backup is sufficient. If you work with important documents regularly, daily backups are recommended. The key is consistency — an automated schedule removes the risk of forgetting."
  - question: "Is cloud backup enough, or do I also need a local backup?"
    answer: "Cloud backup alone can work for many users, but following the 3-2-1 rule is best practice: keep 3 copies of your data, on 2 different media types, with 1 stored offsite (like the cloud). A local backup gives you faster recovery, while cloud backup protects against physical disasters."
  - question: "What files should I prioritize in my backup?"
    answer: "Focus on files that cannot be replaced: personal documents, photos, videos, financial records, passwords (encrypted), and project files. Operating system files and installed applications can usually be reinstalled, so they are lower priority."
  - question: "Are free backup solutions reliable?"
    answer: "Yes, several free tools are reliable for personal use. Windows File History, macOS Time Machine, and free tiers of services like Google Drive or Backblaze Personal Backup (free trial) are all solid options. For most home users, free tools are more than adequate."
